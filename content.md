# Content

Designing Infrastructure for New Buildings: You are developing a concept for newly constructed high-rises that utilizes a centralized rooftop "Flight Deck" and an internal vertical delivery shaft. You refined this system to use a hovering winch-and-drop mechanism into an open catchment funnel, rather than requiring drones to land.

Retrofitting Historic Buildings: You are creating an add-on solution called the "Exo-Logistics Spine" to bring autonomous delivery to older architecture. This involves a modular exterior tube that acts as a pneumatic decelerator to safely drop packages into automatic floor switchers.

Solving Engineering Constraints: You are actively debating and solving real-world physical constraints, such as keeping rain out of the cargo shafts using a passive drainage "S-Trap". You also pivoted your ground-level designs to accommodate small, six-wheeled sidewalk micro-rovers by using specialized intake hatches and precision robotic arms.

Emphasizing Passive Logistics: To keep costs and complexity down, you intentionally avoided expensive robotic arms in the main building core, instead relying on gravity chutes, motorized roller beds, and mechanical divertersi and claws.
